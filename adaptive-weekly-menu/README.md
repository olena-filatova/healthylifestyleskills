# Adaptive Weekly Menu Skill

A Claude skill that builds a realistic, healthy weekly meal plan tailored to your actual life — your health conditions, location, schedule, cooking ability, and budget. Not an idealised version of how you *should* eat, but a practical plan you'll actually follow.

## What it does

The skill runs a short intake conversation to understand your situation before suggesting anything. It then produces a full weekly meal plan with a batch-cooking strategy, shopping list, and an explanation of why the plan works for your specific needs.

It's designed to handle real complexity — Type 1 or Type 2 diabetes, high cholesterol, perimenopause, IBS, weight goals, family households, shift workers, people who hate cooking, tight budgets, and everything in between.

**The output always includes:**
- A weekly table (Breakfast / Lunch / Dinner / Snacks / Notes)
- A batch-cooking and leftovers strategy
- A categorised shopping list
- A plain-English explanation of how the plan supports your health goals

## How to install

1. Download `adaptive-weekly-menu.skill`
2. Open Claude (desktop app)
3. Go to **Settings → Skills → Install from file**
4. Select the `.skill` file

## How to use

Just ask Claude to help you plan your meals for the week. For example:

> *"Can you help me plan my meals for the week?"*
> *"I want to eat better — can you build me a weekly menu?"*
> *"Help me sort out my food for the week, I've got diabetes and I'm trying to keep it simple."*

Claude will ask you a few questions first (location, health conditions, diet preferences, cooking habits, budget, goals) and then build a plan around your answers.

## Who it's designed for

- People managing chronic health conditions (diabetes, high cholesterol, IBS, etc.)
- Anyone who wants to reduce cooking time through batch cooking and freezing
- Households cooking for one, for two, or for a family with different dietary needs
- People in the UK, Ireland, or anywhere — the skill adapts to your local shops and seasonal produce

## What it won't do

- It won't jump straight to a meal plan without asking you questions first
- It won't recommend extreme diets, cleanses, or supplements
- It won't replace advice from your GP or a registered dietitian for serious medical conditions
